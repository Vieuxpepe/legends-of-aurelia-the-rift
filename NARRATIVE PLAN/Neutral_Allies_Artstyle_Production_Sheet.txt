# Legends of Aurelia - Neutral Allies Artstyle Production Sheet

Purpose: Production-facing art direction for named neutral allies and coalition-side non-roster supporters.
Scope: Unique neutral allies only. Excludes generic neutral faction troops and unnamed civic NPCs.

## 1) Global Neutral Ally Visual Pillars

- Base style: gritty dark-fantasy pixel art consistent with campaign readability at tactical zoom.
- Ally read priority: each neutral ally must read as "trusted but independent" rather than core roster uniformity.
- Silhouette rule: practical identity first (trade, clergy, road, court, camp), then faction accent language.
- Material grounding: leather, worn steel, cloth weave, stamped insignia, hand-kept equipment; avoid over-ornate hero treatment.
- Emotional read: neutral allies should look lived-in and world-anchored, with visual evidence of duty, scarcity, and compromise.

## 2) Neutral Ally Hierarchy

- Battlefield neutrals: strongest readability, clear weapon/role silhouette, stronger contrast accents.
- Conditional political allies: refined silhouette with power symbols (seals, brooches, insignia), restrained combat exaggeration.
- Hub/logistics allies: grounded silhouettes with tool identity and low-noise palettes.
- Dangerous allies: sharper contrast and edge-light language, but still readable as allied when active.

## 3) Visual Language by Ally Archetype

- Local wardens/defenders: weathered blues, moss greens, mud browns, practical straps and field patchwork.
- Reformist clergy/moderates: soft ivory, ash blue, desaturated gold, humane iconography rather than punitive motifs.
- League reformists/political brokers: burgundy-charcoal, polished metal trims, document/seal details.
- Mercenary/free company: iron grey, muted red, dust tones, pragmatic asymmetry and campaign wear.
- Sanctuary/civic allies: warm lantern ambers, parchment neutrals, worn linen and stitched repairs.

## 5) Battlefield Readability Rules (Neutral Allies)

- Friendly clarity: neutral allies must keep readable ally-coded edge accents even when politically dangerous.
- Weapon-first legibility: spear, bow, staff, blade, or tool silhouette should be instantly visible.
- Contrast discipline: keep midtone separation from enemy elites; avoid dark-value collapse with busy backgrounds.
- FX restraint: ally VFX should support readability and intent, never mask tile occupancy or action telegraphs.

## 6) Accessibility and Variant Notes

- Minimum palette variant pass per ally: default + high-contrast outline-safe pass.
- Maintain 1px/2px readability checks for tactical zoom and stream compression.
- Test each ally against desert, marsh, ruin, and urban map backgrounds before final approval.
- Keep signal colors consistent with your cursor/state language to reduce player cognitive load.

## 7) Out of Scope

- Generic neutral infantry/civilians.
- Temporary map-only unnamed escorts without narrative identity.
- Full portrait expression sheets (handled in character portrait pipeline).
