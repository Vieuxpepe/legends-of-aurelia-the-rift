A lot, actually. Now that Camp Explore has requests + special scenes + lore beats, you’ve got the bones of a really strong living hub. The best additions are the ones that make camp feel like it changes with the war instead of being a static menu wearing a fake mustache.

Best next additions
1. Character-to-character camp moments

Not just “you talk to Nyx,” but:

Nyx and Kaelen arguing quietly by the fire

Branik and Tamsin sharing a darkly funny exchange

Corvin unsettling someone by being a little too right

These could unlock when:

both characters are in roster

both meet a bond threshold

a story milestone is reached

This is probably the highest value next step, because it makes the cast feel interconnected rather than orbiting only the player.

2. Reactive camp after major battles

After specific chapters, camp mood changes:

different idle lines

different music layer

different firelight / weather / ambient FX

some characters unavailable because they are brooding, praying, repairing gear, etc.

Examples:

after a betrayal chapter, people cluster differently

after a victory, camp is more relaxed, but some characters are suspiciously quiet

after a brutal loss, certain characters only give short responses

That gives real narrative weight without needing giant cutscenes.

3. Camp rumors / overheard conversations

When walking near groups, you catch:

gossip

half-truths

foreshadowing

conflicting opinions on recent events

This is fantastic because it can deliver:

worldbuilding

humor

tension

misdirection

Very cheap compared to full dialogue scenes, and it makes the camp feel inhabited.

4. Relationship-based micro-barks

Tiny contextual lines when you approach a character:

cold if bond is low

familiar if bond is medium

vulnerable or teasing if bond is high

Not full dialogue, just one-liners on proximity or first click.
That adds a surprising amount of life for very little code.

5. Camp state changes by chapter milestone

Make the hub physically evolve:

Greyspire gets more repaired

new tents, banners, workstations, shrines, barricades

more civilians, recruits, quartermasters, mess activity

That makes progression visible in the world instead of only in menus.

Strong medium-sized additions
6. One-time “threshold confessions”

Different from lore beats.

Lore beats are worldview / history.
Confessions are more personal:

regret

fear

guilt

hope

admiration

anger

These should be rarer and higher-threshold:

close

bonded

late-story flags

This is where the emotional gut punches live.

7. Character routines in camp

Instead of static walkers:

Kaelen trains at dawn

Tamsin works near the infirmary

Sorrel reads by lamplight

Branik near the cookfire

Hest constantly in the wrong place, probably holding a dangerous object

Even simple routine zones make camp feel dramatically more alive.

8. Camp event nights

Small rotating events:

sparring ring

memorial vigil

shared meal

repairs after a siege

storytelling around the fire

storm shelter night

These can act as lightweight scene generators and let multiple characters participate.

9. Lore codex unlocked from camp

When a lore beat is consumed, add it to:

a character journal

camp recollections

faction notes

Optional, but useful if players want to revisit what they learned without re-triggering scenes.

10. Small gift / token system

Nothing huge or dating-sim-ish. Just:

a few specific items matter to certain characters

giving one unlocks a short line, trust bump, or lore beat

Very easy to overdo, so keep it restrained. But it can work well if tied to character identity.

Big but excellent additions
11. Camp disputes you can influence

Two characters clash over:

strategy

morality

faith

risk

loyalty

You choose how to respond, and it affects:

trust

future camp scenes

maybe even battle bonuses or penalties

This is one of the best ways to make the player feel like they’re leading people, not just collecting them.

12. Camp memory chains

A beat from early game gets referenced later.

Example:

Nyx mentions “messages that arrive quietly”

much later, after a major death or betrayal, she references that old conversation again

That kind of callback makes the writing feel authored, not generated out of a soup cauldron.

13. Pair bond evolution

Certain duos develop over time:

rivalry

mutual respect

protectiveness

ideological friction

These can unlock:

duo camp scenes

support-style banter

paired battle bonuses later

Small features with high payoff

If you want efficient wins, I’d prioritize these:

Character-to-character camp scenes

Reactive post-battle camp mood/state

Rumors / overheard dialogue

Micro-barks based on relationship tier

Camp visual evolution by chapter

That combo would make the hub feel dramatically richer without building a giant new subsystem from scratch.

My strongest recommendation

If you want the single best next feature, do this:

paired camp scenes between characters, gated by story progress and bond thresholds

Because that gives you:

more worldbuilding

more cast chemistry

more emotional payoff

less “everyone only talks to the player” syndrome

And that syndrome, frankly, is one of the great hidden banana peels of party-based RPG writing.
