## Step 1: Enemy roster balance strategy

I would split the 20 enemies into four gameplay families:

**1. Scripted terror / nonstandard bosses**
Vespera on Level 1, Vespera Ascendant, and the Witness should not play like normal “kill boss” units. They need phase rules, capped damage windows, map pressure, and ritual anchors. That matches their intended role as reality-warping or cosmic threats.  

**2. Recurring rivals who retreat**
Caldris, Rhex, Mortivar, Cassian, Nerez, and Selene should usually **escape before full defeat** on earlier maps. They are stronger as long-term faces than as one-and-done stat blocks. Your file already frames several of them that way. 

**3. Chapter bosses with one strong map identity**
Ephrem, Juno, Septen, Edda, Halwen, Noemi, Serik, Roen, Ash Adjudicator, Alric, Naeva, and Enric should each represent one dominant pressure axis: zeal, alarms, famine logistics, forest control, arena spectacle, infiltration, lockdown, betrayal, trials, cavalry command, ritual tide, and marsh necromancy.  

**4. Pressure distribution rule**
Do not stack too many “miserable” mechanics too early.
Early game should introduce:

* one untouchable horror,
* one pursuit controller,
* one zeal bruiser,
* one reinforcement economy boss.

Midgame can add stealth, extractions, alarms, and lockout tools.
Late game is where multi-boss maps and spatial manipulation should peak. This lines up well with your map-by-map placement.

## Tight balancing notes

A few tuning choices matter a lot:

**Do not let Caldris fully anti-heal-lock the player on Level 2.**
Her early version should hit only a small cross or one lane.

**Rhex should be dangerous because he buys time, not because he personally one-rounds units.**
His threat comes from reinforcements and map tempo.

**Mortivar must be durable, but slow enough that positioning still beats him.**
If he becomes both wall and sweeper too early, he invalidates tactical play.

**Noemi and Selene should kill careless backliners, not everybody.**
Their danger should reward formation discipline.

**Ash Adjudicator and Witness should be phase bosses, not raw-stat bricks.**
Their identity is stronger when the player solves conditions, not just DPS races.

**Vespera should always feel unfair in a deliberate, authored way.**
Not random unfairness—ritual unfairness.
