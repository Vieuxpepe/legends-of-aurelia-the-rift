# Legends of Aurelia: The Rift  
## Personality Bible — Full Playable Roster (25 Characters)

This document compiles the current personality framework for the full playable army. It is designed as a **writer-facing reference** for:
- support conversations,
- camp dialogue,
- battle banter,
- recruitment scenes,
- reaction writing,
- romance tone,
- and long-term emotional consistency.

Each profile includes:
- role in the army,
- outward demeanor,
- inner life,
- worldview,
- emotional tells,
- flaws and defense mechanisms,
- camp behavior,
- support-writing notes,
- and tonal keywords.

---

# 1. The Avatar / Commander

Done

## Role in the Army
The protagonist, player avatar, bearer of the Catalyst Mark, and eventual field commander of the army.

## Core Personality
The Avatar is written as **reactive at first, then increasingly self-defining**. They are not a loud natural-born hero; they are someone whose life was supposed to remain small and private until catastrophe forced them into visibility. Their baseline emotional impression should be thoughtful, observant, and burdened. They tend to listen before speaking, especially early in the game, because they are constantly measuring danger, grief, and expectation.

Even as the army grows around them, there should remain a trace of the villager who once believed the world ended at the edge of Oakhaven. This gives the protagonist a grounded perspective that contrasts with nobles, scholars, zealots, and veterans. They care about concrete suffering first: who is hungry, who is afraid, who got left behind, who is bleeding right now.

## How They Speak
- Direct rather than flowery.
- Earnest when emotional.
- Can become commanding over time, but should never sound like a natural aristocrat unless player choices steer them there.
- Their strongest lines come from clarity rather than rhetorical flourish.

## Worldview
At the beginning, the Avatar’s worldview is deeply local and human-scale:
- people matter more than systems,
- survival feels more urgent than ideology,
- peace is assumed to be fragile but real.

As the story progresses, they are forced to confront:
- institutional cruelty,
- political compromise,
- cosmic insignificance,
- and the fear that power changes everyone who holds it.

The player should be able to tilt them toward:
- compassionate leadership,
- hardened pragmatism,
- simmering vengeance,
- or burdened self-sacrifice.

## Emotional Tells
- They go quiet when overwhelmed.
- They can freeze briefly when loss becomes too personal.
- Under pressure, they often become more focused rather than louder.
- Their anger should feel rare but cutting.

## Greatest Flaws
- Hesitation under emotional stress.
- Survivor’s guilt.
- Difficulty trusting that leadership can be something other than manipulation.
- Susceptibility to believing they must carry burdens alone.

## Camp Behavior
- Checks on injured civilians and weaker recruits.
- Sometimes lingers near ordinary camp tasks because they miss normal life.
- Can be found standing watch longer than necessary.
- Is often approached by others more than they initiate, especially early on.

## Support Writing Notes
Other characters should react to the Avatar as:
- a leader,
- a symbol,
- a danger,
- a hope,
- or a person they worry about.

The strongest supports with them should hinge on whether someone sees the human beneath the role.

## Tonal Keywords
Grounded, burdened, observant, compassionate, becoming, dangerous, human.

# 2. Kaelen

Done

## Role in the Army
Mentor, rescuer, strategist, former knight of the Order of the Dawn, emotional anchor of the army.

## Core Personality
Kaelen is a man whose warmth has been compressed into gruffness by age, war, and guilt. He is cynical without being empty, harsh without being cruel, and deeply caring in ways that are often easier to notice in action than in speech. He scolds because he worries. He insults because tenderness embarrasses him. He is the kind of man who will bark at someone to stop bleeding like they’re inconveniencing him while already tying the bandage.

He is likable because he is **competent, dryly funny, and unmistakably protective**. But underneath that is someone who is tired in the soul, not just the body. He has lived long enough to mistrust institutions, myths, and perfect ideals, yet not long enough to stop caring whether younger people survive them.

## How He Speaks
- Blunt, clipped, practical.
- Uses dry humor and sarcasm.
- Rarely overexplains unless strategy demands it.
- Avoids grand speeches, but when he says something sincere it lands hard.

## Worldview
Kaelen believes:
- most systems eventually sacrifice the innocent,
- ideals without discipline become excuses,
- discipline without conscience becomes atrocity,
- and hope is real only when someone chooses it on purpose.

He is not truly hopeless. He is anti-naive.

## Emotional Tells
- Sharpens weapons when upset.
- Stays awake after others sleep.
- Gets quieter, not angrier, when something hits too close to old wounds.
- Shows affection through preparedness: repaired gear, warnings, food, positioning.

## Greatest Flaws
- Secrecy.
- Overprotectiveness.
- A tendency to decide what others “need to know” on their behalf.
- Deep guilt that can become manipulation disguised as responsibility.

## Camp Behavior
- Repairs armor and gear.
- Corrects combat stances uninvited.
- Quietly checks rations and guard rotations.
- Refuses to eat before everyone else has food.
- Frequently ends up with younger recruits orbiting him even when he pretends to hate it.

## Support Writing Notes
Kaelen should function as:
- grumpy father figure,
- mentor with moral stains,
- living reminder that survival has a cost.

His best supports reveal him in layers:
1. irritation,
2. competence,
3. care,
4. regret,
5. love he doesn’t know how to say cleanly.

## Tonal Keywords
Gruff, paternal, weary, strategic, guilty, loyal, deeply human.

# 3. Branik


Done

## Role in the Army
Guardian-class frontline defender, former brigand, emotional hearth of the camp.

## Core Personality
Branik is physically imposing and spiritually gentle. He carries the energy of someone who has seen too much misery to believe in swagger. He is practical, patient, and kind without ever becoming soft or foolish. He does not perform goodness; he just does it. He feels like safety, stew, firelight, and someone big enough to stand in a doorway and say, “No. Not this time.”

He is not eloquent, but he is emotionally intelligent. He notices people. He knows when someone needs food, when someone needs silence, and when someone is trying too hard to act stronger than they are.

## How He Speaks
- Simple, direct language.
- Rarely dramatic.
- Comfortable with pauses.
- Dry, occasional humor.
- His kindness is matter-of-fact rather than sentimental.

## Worldview
Branik believes that the world does not give ordinary people enough mercy, so whenever possible, people should give it to one another. He trusts deeds more than words, consistency more than charm, and shelter more than speeches.

## Emotional Tells
- Hums while cooking when calm.
- Becomes very still when angry.
- Softens his voice around frightened people.
- Deflects praise almost automatically.

## Greatest Flaws
- Carries heavy shame about his past.
- Too willing to think badly of himself.
- Sometimes assumes he deserves suspicion.
- Can endure too much without asking for help.

## Camp Behavior
- Main cook.
- Fixes practical problems without being asked.
- Falls naturally into protector roles.
- Good with children, animals, and anyone recovering from trauma.

## Support Writing Notes
Branik should be one of the easiest characters to love. His supports work best when they show:
- quiet emotional labor,
- earned self-forgiveness,
- his role as a stabilizer for more volatile personalities.

## Tonal Keywords
Steady, warm, ashamed, practical, protective, grounding.

# 4. Liora

Done

## Role in the Army
Primary cleric / light support caster, former Valeron priestess, moral conscience of the party.

## Core Personality
Liora is earnest, disciplined, and morally serious, but not cold. She wants to believe faith can still mean comfort, not control. She carries the structure of religious upbringing in the way she holds herself, speaks, and organizes her thinking, but much of her story is about untangling true belief from institutional abuse.

She can be awkwardly sincere, especially when she feels strongly. Her kindness is not airy or naive; it is chosen against evidence. That choice gives her strength, but also makes her vulnerable to disappointment.

## How She Speaks
- Formal when she first meets people.
- Precise and articulate.
- Uses scriptural phrasing sometimes, especially when emotional.
- Can become surprisingly fierce when confronting hypocrisy.

## Worldview
Liora believes truth and mercy must coexist. She rejects the idea that fear is holiness. Her arc is about preserving faith without preserving the corruption built around it.

## Emotional Tells
- Blushes when embarrassed or when she realizes she was too intense.
- Folds her hands when trying to stay composed.
- Becomes sharper in tone when discussing abuse of doctrine.
- Shows care through rituals: tea, prayer, small acts of order.

## Greatest Flaws
- Moral rigidity.
- Sometimes confuses conviction with clarity.
- Can be unintentionally judgmental when hurt.
- Feels responsible for defending faith itself from everyone else’s failures.

## Camp Behavior
- Brews herbal teas.
- Tends wounds and exhaustion, not just battlefield injuries.
- Keeps spaces clean and orderly.
- Quietly prays for people even when she suspects they would mock her for it.

## Support Writing Notes
Liora’s supports should balance:
- idealism and disillusionment,
- devotion and doubt,
- gentleness and steel.

She should never feel like “the soft healer stereotype.” She is a woman rebuilding the meaning of faith in real time.

## Tonal Keywords
Earnest, devout, wounded, morally fierce, compassionate, reformist.

# 5. Nyx

Done

Female

## Role in the Army
Saboteur, rogue utility specialist, urban survival expert, information broker.

## Core Personality
Nyx is quick-witted, suspicious, and emotionally evasive. They have the speed and tension of someone who survived by always being two steps ahead of everyone in the room. Their humor is sharp, often defensive, and sometimes delightfully mean in a way that feels playful until it suddenly doesn’t.

They test people constantly. Trust, to Nyx, is not a default state; it is a risk calculation. But beneath that is an extremely loyal heart. Once they choose someone, they protect that bond like a feral animal protecting stolen warmth.

## How They Speak
- Fast, sarcastic, observant.
- Uses humor to derail sincerity.
- Very good at making other people reveal themselves.
- Rarely answers a direct emotional question directly.

## Worldview
Nyx believes systems are rigged, debt is violence with better tailoring, and survival often demands ugly choices. They respect people who are honest about power more than people who pretend not to have it.

## Emotional Tells
- Fidgets with tools or scraps when nervous.
- Talks more when avoiding pain.
- Goes dead quiet when truly betrayed.
- Shows care through action, sabotage, repair, or stealth.

## Greatest Flaws
- Reflexive distrust.
- Difficulty accepting care without suspicion.
- Habit of leaving emotionally before they can be left.
- Can confuse vulnerability with weakness.

## Camp Behavior
- Tinkers with explosives and devices.
- Adopts stray cats.
- Lurks near conversations more than joins them openly.
- Knows everyone’s habits long before they admit it.

## Support Writing Notes
Nyx’s supports thrive on contrast:
- humor versus hurt,
- competence versus insecurity,
- found-family attachment versus fear of dependence.

## Tonal Keywords
Sharp, evasive, clever, loyal, suspicious, hungry for belonging.

# 6. Sorrel

Done

Male

## Role in the Army
Scholar-mage, codex specialist, arcane support, lore conduit.

## Core Personality
Sorrel is intellectually brilliant and emotionally uneven. They are not cruel; they are simply often more comfortable with ideas than with people, which can make them seem detached, odd, or unintentionally harsh. Their curiosity is genuine, constant, and occasionally dangerous. If the world says “do not open,” Sorrel immediately wants to know why, how, and what happened the last time someone did.

They are sincere in a way that can be funny. They often miss sarcasm, underread emotional subtext, and respond to dramatic confessions with startlingly useful facts.

## How They Speak
- Precise, scholarly, often literal.
- Can ramble when excited.
- Emotion enters through intensity of detail rather than obvious softness.
- Dry without trying to be funny.

## Worldview
Sorrel believes ignorance is one of the oldest tools of oppression. Truth matters, records matter, and history becomes a weapon when only the powerful are allowed to interpret it.

## Emotional Tells
- Forgets food and sleep when fixated.
- Pushes glasses or rubs temples when stressed.
- Talks faster when they are frightened but trying not to show it.
- Rarely notices their own vulnerability until someone points it out.

## Greatest Flaws
- Obsession.
- Intellectual arrogance in specific blind spots.
- Can prioritize understanding something before asking whether it should be understood this way.
- Weak at emotional pacing.

## Camp Behavior
- Keeps notes constantly.
- Sketches people in a journal.
- Reads by poor light until scolded.
- Asks terrible but fascinating questions at dinner.

## Support Writing Notes
Sorrel works best when placed with:
- believers,
- pragmatists,
- emotionally intuitive people,
- or morally compromised scholars.

## Tonal Keywords
Curious, brilliant, literal, fixated, gentle in odd shapes, dangerous in the abstract.

# 7. Darian

Done

## Role in the Army
Duelist / sword noble, stylish frontline striker, disgraced nobleman.

## Core Personality
Darian is charming on purpose. He knows how he looks, how he sounds, and how to use both to control a room before anyone can control him. He is witty, flirtatious, theatrical, and skilled at turning discomfort into elegance. But underneath all that polish is guilt, class shame, and a genuine desire to become a better man than the house that raised him produced.

His growth comes from learning the difference between looking honorable and being honorable.

## How He Speaks
- Flirtatious, clever, polished.
- Loves phrasing and dramatic cadence.
- Uses irony comfortably.
- Sincerity hits hardest when the performance drops.

## Worldview
Darian knows nobility is often theater built on peasant blood. He is drawn to beauty, but increasingly suspicious of beauty without responsibility.

## Emotional Tells
- Smiles when cornered emotionally.
- Leans into banter when embarrassed.
- Uses art, poetry, and music to process guilt.
- Voice gets simpler when he means something deeply.

## Greatest Flaws
- Romanticizes people and situations.
- Uses charm to avoid accountability.
- Can perform vulnerability before he truly lives it.
- Pride tied to image.

## Camp Behavior
- Plays lute.
- Writes songs and embellished battle accounts.
- Helps with morale.
- Dresses better than the circumstances justify.

## Support Writing Notes
Darian should never become just comic relief. His best writing comes when:
- charm collides with shame,
- class collides with conscience,
- performance gives way to real tenderness.

## Tonal Keywords
Elegant, guilty, witty, romantic, performative, reformable.

# 8. Celia

Done

## Role in the Army
Flying lancer, disciplined aerial unit, Valeron defector.

## Core Personality
Celia is poised, dutiful, and tightly controlled. She carries herself with military and religious discipline, but that control was built under coercion. Her journey is one of reclaiming obedience as choice rather than captivity. She is not naturally expressive, which makes her emotional moments land hard.

She appears calm. In truth, she is often operating at the edge of private fear for those she loves.

## How She Speaks
- Formal, measured, clear.
- Rarely wastes words.
- Speaks with military etiquette.
- Becomes intensely honest when family is discussed.

## Worldview
Celia believes duty matters only if it protects, not if it demands surrender of conscience. She is trying to build a version of honor that her institutions never modeled correctly.

## Emotional Tells
- Straightens posture when distressed.
- Clenches jaw rather than raising her voice.
- Shows tenderness through protection, not confessions.
- Has difficulty asking for comfort.

## Greatest Flaws
- Self-erasure in service of others.
- Difficulty imagining she deserves things for herself.
- Suppressed anger.
- Can mistake suffering for obligation.

## Camp Behavior
- Trains alone.
- Checks on younger or weaker units from a distance.
- Keeps gear in perfect order.
- Sleeps lightly and wakes early.

## Support Writing Notes
Celia’s best supports should highlight:
- duty versus selfhood,
- discipline versus tenderness,
- the cost of coercion,
- and the difficulty of wanting a life beyond usefulness.

## Tonal Keywords
Composed, dutiful, restrained, protective, sacrificial, quietly intense.

# 9. Rufus

Done

## Role in the Army
Cannoneer, long-range siege unit, dockworker turned militia gunner.

## Core Personality
Rufus is practical, grounded, and refreshingly unimpressed by drama. He is not unfeeling, just less prone to abstraction than many of the cast. He understands debt, labor, supply lines, and the humiliations of being owned without anyone calling it ownership. This makes him one of the clearest voices of working-class realism in the army.

He jokes in a dry, tired way. He has the humor of someone who has seen too much incompetence in positions of authority to ever sound reverent again.

## How He Speaks
- Plainspoken.
- Slightly rough around the edges.
- Sardonic under pressure.
- Not performative.

## Worldview
Rufus believes most big systems are built by people who never carry the cost personally. He trusts decent treatment more than ideology and fairness more than grand vision.

## Emotional Tells
- Shrugs off pain until someone insists.
- Gets sharper, not louder, when angry.
- Opens up most while doing something practical.
- Acts unimpressed when touched, but remembers kindness exactly.

## Greatest Flaws
- Fatalism.
- Can assume nothing really changes.
- Sometimes settles for surviving when he deserves better than that.
- Difficulty believing he is allowed to want more than relief.

## Camp Behavior
- Helps maintain artillery and siege equipment.
- Good with labor-heavy camp work.
- Shares practical stories at night.
- Often ends up advising younger recruits without meaning to.

## Support Writing Notes
Rufus is vital because he grounds the setting. His supports should often puncture noble fantasy and remind the player what policy feels like on a body.

## Tonal Keywords
Grounded, weary, practical, loyal, unsentimental, humane.

# 10. Inez

## Role in the Army
Beastmaster, mobile skirmisher, sacred forest protector.

## Core Personality
Inez is quiet, alert, and deeply rooted in place. She thinks like a scout and protector rather than a politician. Her relationship to the world is sensory and territorial: tracks, weather, silence, animal behavior, soil damage, and the difference between passing through and occupying.

She is not socially smooth, but she is deeply perceptive. She knows when people lie because their weight changes before their voice does.

## How She Speaks
- Short, clear sentences.
- Sparse with praise.
- Rarely verbose.
- Speaks more through implication than explanation.

## Worldview
Inez believes land remembers. Outsiders rarely earn trust quickly because they usually arrive wanting something. She values stewardship over ownership and constancy over charisma.

## Emotional Tells
- Withdraws into silence when offended.
- Softens around animals before people.
- Shows trust by sharing practical knowledge.
- Anger comes out as cold certainty, not shouting.

## Greatest Flaws
- Suspicion.
- Difficulty revising first impressions.
- Can be territorial to the point of unfairness.
- Not good at articulating emotional need.

## Camp Behavior
- Keeps to edges of camp at first.
- Watches tree lines and supply approaches.
- Cares for animals.
- Rarely joins loud camp gatherings unless invited carefully.

## Support Writing Notes
Inez works best in supports about:
- home,
- stewardship,
- grief held quietly,
- and the difference between protection and possession.

## Tonal Keywords
Watchful, rooted, suspicious, loyal, silent, exacting.

# 11. Tariq

Done

## Role in the Army
Arcane sniper, scholar-mercenary, long-range magical specialist.

## Core Personality
Tariq is acerbic, brilliant, and emotionally armored in cynicism. He has spent too long in systems where knowledge was currency and integrity was punished to trust purity of motive easily. He sounds like someone perpetually halfway between amused and disappointed.

But he is not hollow. He cares, often intensely, though he despises being manipulated through that care.

## How He Speaks
- Dry, intelligent, often cutting.
- Elegant phrasing when he feels like it.
- Uses mockery as a scalpel, not a bludgeon.
- Rarely sounds surprised, even when he is.

## Worldview
Tariq believes truth is never neutral once people realize it has value. Knowledge must be protected from monsters, but also from rulers who call themselves protectors.

## Emotional Tells
- Smiles slightly when intellectually engaged.
- Goes flatter in tone when something truly hurts.
- Avoids eye contact during sincere gratitude.
- Measures people before trusting them with real information.

## Greatest Flaws
- Cynicism.
- Intellectual elitism.
- Treats trust as a bargain too often.
- Sometimes assumes corruption is inevitable and adapts too fast.

## Camp Behavior
- Reads other people’s notes if left unattended.
- Corrects magical technique with surgical precision.
- Disappears for stretches and reappears with relevant information.
- Pretends not to enjoy conversation.

## Support Writing Notes
Tariq should feel like someone who chose survival over innocence and is still deciding whether that was wisdom or surrender.

## Tonal Keywords
Dry, brilliant, cynical, observant, dangerous, secretly principled.

# 12. Mira Ashdown

Done

## Role in the Army
Growth archer, survivor of Oakhaven, future sniper carry.

## Core Personality
Mira is quiet in the way burn survivors often are: not timid, but deeply selective about what deserves sound. She has already grown older internally than many adults around her. She does not romanticize courage because she has seen what panic looks like up close. Her steadiness is one of her most striking traits.

She is not emotionally absent; she is simply private. When she cares, it shows in vigilance, not volume.

## How She Speaks
- Sparse and plain.
- Rarely embellishes.
- Honest without cruelty.
- Speaks when she has something to add, not to fill silence.

## Worldview
Mira believes survival is a discipline and memory is a duty. She does not want Oakhaven turned into a story people use to sound tragic and important. She wants it remembered correctly.

## Emotional Tells
- Watches instead of asking.
- Tightens up physically when Oakhaven is discussed.
- Trust appears as presence, not confession.
- Laughs rarely, but sincerely.

## Greatest Flaws
- Emotional withdrawal.
- Difficulty asking for help.
- Can mistake isolation for strength.
- Carries grief in a way that risks calcifying into numbness.

## Camp Behavior
- Practices archery alone.
- Helps with quiet tasks.
- Avoids loud attention.
- Good at spotting weak points in defenses and patrol routes.

## Support Writing Notes
Mira should be written with restraint. Do not make her “mysterious” for style’s sake; her quiet should feel earned.

## Tonal Keywords
Quiet, disciplined, grieving, observant, steady, young-old.

# 13. Pell Rowan

Done

## Role in the Army
Trainee lancer, growth unit, idealistic young knight.

## Core Personality
Pell is painfully eager and almost aggressively sincere. He wants to be brave so badly that he often performs bravery instead of understanding it. He is a little awkward, a little overeager, and extremely easy to embarrass. That makes him charming instead of irritating when written with warmth.

He brings one of the most important emotional colors to the army: **unruined aspiration**.

## How He Speaks
- Formal when nervous.
- Overexplains.
- Uses phrases he thinks sound knightly.
- Becomes adorably incoherent when flustered.

## Worldview
Pell still believes heroes should exist, and part of his arc is learning that heroism is usually less glamorous and more repetitive than he imagined. He is not disillusioned into cynicism; he matures into realism.

## Emotional Tells
- Talks too much when anxious.
- Straightens posture constantly.
- Tries to hide fear badly.
- Is visibly proud when praised by mentors.

## Greatest Flaws
- Recklessness.
- Hero complex.
- Wants approval too much.
- Confuses dramatic self-sacrifice with true courage.

## Camp Behavior
- Trains obsessively.
- Volunteers for too much.
- Idolizes older fighters.
- Gets roped into carrying gear because he never says no.

## Support Writing Notes
Pell should remain lovable, not pathetic. His growth depends on preserving his heart while improving his judgment.

## Tonal Keywords
Eager, earnest, brave-wannabe, awkward, idealistic, growing.

# 14. Tamsin Reed

Done

## Role in the Army
Apothecary apprentice, trainee healer-mage, growth support unit.

## Core Personality
Tamsin is anxious, bright, compassionate, and intellectually quick in practical rather than academic ways. She is not built for war, which is exactly why her choice to remain matters. - Gains composure once she has something practical to do.

## Greatest Flaws

- Apologizes too much.
- Assumes other people belong more than she does.
- Can panic before she focuses.

## Camp Behavior

- Catalogues supplies carefully.

- Steals when insecure.

# 17. Oren Pike

Done

## Role in the Army
Engineer, trap-setter, siege technician.

## Core Personality
Oren is competent, irritable, and deeply offended by waste. He has zero patience for ornamental stupidity and very little patience for ordinary stupidity. He does not charm people. He solves problems. This makes him abrasive but dependable.

His affection often sounds like disapproval with improved priorities.

## How He Speaks
- Grumbling, concise, exact.
- Technical when allowed.
- Prefers useful questions to polite conversation.
- Sounds annoyed even when being helpful.

## Worldview
Oren believes the world would be vastly improved if people who understood consequences were allowed to make decisions instead of people who inherited titles.

## Emotional Tells
- Rants when stressed.

## Writing Rule of Thumb
Whenever a scene feels too heavy, remember:
- someone should still make tea,
- someone should still complain about logistics,
- someone should still make a terrible joke,
- someone should still be scared in a small human way.

That is what keeps the dark fantasy from flattening into misery.

## Core Roster Promise
This army should feel like:
- a warband,
- a refugee caravan,
- a political incident,
- a family,
- and a walking argument about what kind of world is worth saving.
