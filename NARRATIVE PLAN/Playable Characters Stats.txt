## Step 1: Roster balance strategy

I treated this roster like a full campaign army, not a pile of individually cool units.

The army is deliberately **not symmetrical**. It leans physical early, then slowly unlocks magical precision, mobility spikes, and niche utility. That gives the early game a harsher, grounded feel and prevents the player from solving every map with the same 6 units.

### Role distribution

* **Early stability / crutch units:** Kaelen, Branik, Liora
  These keep the opening chapters from becoming pure attrition, but only Kaelen is a true “Jagen.”
* **True project units / bloomers:** Mira, Pell, Tamsin, Hest
  These start noticeably undercooked and become excellent only if protected and fed.
* **Mid-game carries:** Darian, Celia, Tariq, Garrick, Maela
  These are the units that convert a stable roster into an aggressive one.
* **Pure utility / force multipliers:** Nyx, Yselle, Meris, Oren
  Their value is positional, informational, or tactical more than raw stats.
* **Frontline anchors:** Branik, Alden, Veska, Garrick, Hadrien
  All tanks are not the same: Branik is forgiving, Veska is a wall, Alden is sustain, Garrick is mobile discipline, Hadrien is late-game elite bulk.
* **Siege / range specialists:** Rufus, Oren, Sabine, Tariq, Mira
  This keeps map control varied without giving one bow unit every job.
* **High-risk magical core:** Sorrel, Tariq, Corvin
  All three are strong, but none are forgiving. Sorrel is frail, Tariq is cynical artillery, Corvin is volatile dark efficiency.
* **Fliers with different jobs:** Celia is disciplined and safer; Maela is a speed snowball with real collapse risk.

### Physical vs magical balance

The roster lands at roughly **16 physical bodies** and **9 magical/support/hybrid units**. That is intentional:

* the army can field a frontline,
* but magical damage and healing are still scarce enough to matter,
* and true support slots remain contested.

### Archetypes intentionally covered

* **Jagen:** Kaelen
* **Est / late bloomer:** Tamsin
* **Growth archer carry:** Mira
* **Heroic trainee:** Pell
* **Glass scholar:** Sorrel
* **Dark nuke with poor survivability:** Corvin
* **Dodge tank / utility thief:** Nyx
* **Physical wall:** Veska
* **Gentle bruiser:** Branik
* **Mid/late elite pre-promote:** Hadrien
* **Pure support dancer:** Yselle
* **Honest-stat cavalry:** Garrick

### Important balancing principle

I did **not** flatten weaknesses:

* frail scholars stay frail,
* dancers are not secretly combat gods,
* tanks do not randomly gain speed,
* thief/trickster units do not get hand-holding defense growth,
* and pre-promotes have much lower ceilings than the growth projects.

### Weapon ecosystem fix

Your current weapon array under-supports several playable archetypes:

* bows,
* knives,
* firearms,
* fist weapons,
* dark tomes,
* dancer tools.

So I invented low-tier weapons only where the current list would force a nonsense loadout.

## Final balancing read

This roster should feel healthy in play because:

* early maps are stabilized without being trivialized,
* growth units are real commitments,
* support slots compete with combat slots,
* tanks and fliers are differentiated,
* magic is powerful but brittle,
* and no ascended path erases a fundamentally bad stat profile.

The biggest winners long-term should be **Mira, Pell, Tamsin, Maela, Darian, and Corvin**.
The safest early investments should be **Kaelen, Branik, Liora, Garrick, and Sabine**.
The most punishable bad investments should be **Yselle in combat, Tamsin without protection, Hest if stat-screwed, and any low-bulk unit forced into DawnExalt fantasies**.
