Yes — that’s a **very good system**, and it’s smarter than making villagers only a yes/no recruitment gate.

## Why global fame works well

Saving villagers in Level 1 giving **global fame** does a few great things:

* it makes the player feel that mercy has **world consequences**
* it avoids making the first map too punishing
* it gives you a stateless reward that can matter later without bloating the tutorial
* it helps the campaign feel interconnected

So instead of:

* “you failed the escort puzzle, too bad”

it becomes:

* “you survived, but how much of your humanity and reputation did you preserve?”

That is much better design. Very tasty.

## Titles are an excellent next layer

Yes, adding **titles** later is a really strong idea.

Because fame is broad, while titles are **specific**.

### Example difference:

* **Global Fame** = how widely known/respected/feared the party is
* **Titles** = what the world specifically remembers you for

That gives you two levers:

### Fame answers:

* “How much does the world know your name?”

### Titles answer:

* “What does the world think your name means?”

That distinction is gold for an SRPG campaign.

## Best way to design it

I would **not** make every little action give a title.
That becomes achievement soup.

Instead, make titles:

* rarer
* memorable
* tied to major chapter behavior
* sometimes mutually exclusive

That makes them feel legendary instead of checklist sludge.

---

## A clean structure you could use

### 1. Global Fame

A single running value, maybe:

* negative to positive
* or 0 to 100+
* raised by rescues, public victories, honorable acts
* lowered by cruelty, failures, panic, betrayal, collateral damage

### 2. Hidden Reputation Axes

Optional later:

* **Mercy**
* **Dread**
* **Faith**
* **Pragmatism**
* **Defiance**
* **Legitimacy**

These stay hidden or semi-hidden.

### 3. Titles

Unlocked by combinations of:

* specific map outcomes
* thresholds
* who survived
* who was present
* what choice was made

So a title is not just:
`fame >= 20`

It’s more like:
`Saved 3+ villagers in Level 1 + Kaelen survived + no house looting event triggered`

That makes titles feel earned.

---

## Excellent uses for titles

Titles can unlock:

* hidden shops
* special support conversations
* alternate recruitment conditions
* unique camp dialogue
* extra neutral reinforcements
* secret map routes
* relics or blessings
* special epilogue text
* tower content
* faction-specific reactions

That is way more interesting than just “+5 avoid” slapped on a menu badge.

---

## Best rule for your game

For **Legends of Aurelia**, titles should mostly do **narrative-mechanical unlocking**, not raw stat boosts.

So titles should feel like:

* the world remembers you
* doors open differently
* people interpret you differently
* secrets become reachable

Rather than:

* “Ash-Savior: +2 DEF”

The second one is okay sometimes, but the first one is much richer for your campaign.

## My recommendation

Do this:

* **Use fame now**
* **Track hidden chapter outcome flags now**
* **Add titles later as curated milestone rewards**

That way you don’t block progress, but you also future-proof the system beautifully.

A nice simple formula is:

* Level 1 now tracks:

  * villagers saved
  * fame gained
  * Mira unlock flag

* Later you layer:

  * title unlocks
  * secret reactions
  * hidden content branches

That gives you design flexibility without overbuilding the first chapter into a flaming spreadsheet.

The next strong step would be designing a **Fame / Titles framework** before we continue map-by-map, so each level can plant future flags consistently.
